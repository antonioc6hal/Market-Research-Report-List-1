<p><p>The Digital Visual Interface (DVI) market comprises three main types: DVI-A, DVI-D, and DVI-I. DVI-A transmits analog signals, making it compatible with older displays. DVI-D is designed for digital signals only, ensuring high-quality video transmission without analog conversion. DVI-I combines both analog and digital capabilities, offering flexibility for a variety of monitors. Each type serves specific needs in the market, catering to different display technologies and user requirements, from high-definition video to backward compatibility.</p></p>
